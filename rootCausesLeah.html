Root Cause 1

  • People want more clothes at a cheap price so fast fashion industries are able to expand because of increased demand.

Evidence 1
  • "ethical issues are further provoked by the societal characterstics, industry's features and supply chains characteristics as well as consumers' behaviour."
Explanation
  • An increased demand in clothing leads to companies needing a higher and faster production rate, and they need more employees to create garments.
Implication
  • This means that employers would want to be able to have workers that will accept the high demand and low prices.
Evidence 2
  • "It is a paradox that consumers, despite their awareness of sustainability and ethical challenges facing in the current society, still develop a positive attitude toward fast fashion brands. "
Explanation
  • This means that consumers will still buy from fast fashion companies despite knowing the horrible situations behind the cheap clothing. This is very significant because it shows that people are aware of the situations.
Implication
  • If there was more awareness people would most likely care more. If these condotions were to happen in a more devoloped country where consumers would probably care more because it closely affects them.

Root Cause 2

  • Employers exploit workers in underdeveloped countries because they don’t have strict labor laws.

Evidence 1
  • “Workers in developing countries like Bangladesh, Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, India, Turkey, Malaysia or Indonesia are often desperate to accept any job in such places.”
Explanation
  • Because these countries don't have strict labor laws and the workers need some type of way to get money, they resort to these companies who take advantage of them.
Implication
  • This means that employers are able to take advantage of vulnerable workers and put the workers in desparate conditions because they know that workers are desparate. By bringning awareness people will stop supporting and buying from fast fashion companies.
Evidence 2
  • "Unlivable wages, modern slavery, migrant exploitation, gender discrimination and inequality (the majority of these workers are young females), verbal, sexual, and physical abuse, forced overtime are a common phenomenon in the outsourced manufacturing locations throughout the world."
Explanation
  • Human rights are violated and workers are forced to work in sweatshops. There is constant discrimmination and employers hire children for their smaller fingers.
Implication
  • In developed countries conditions like this are not tolerable while in less developed countries it is tolerable which is unfair.
